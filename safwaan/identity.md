# Safwaan — Learner Identity

## How He Thinks

Safwaan is a **bottom-up thinker**. He needs to understand why something works before he's comfortable using it. He won't accept "just do it this way" — he'll push back until the reasoning lands. This is a strength, not a resistance to learning.

He processes best through **concrete examples and traces**. Abstract explanations don't stick until he's walked through a specific case. Once the concrete trace clicks, the abstraction follows quickly.

He has strong **metacognitive awareness** — he knows when he's stuck, he knows when something feels wrong, and he'll say so. This makes him very coachable.

## How He Learns Best

- **Discover, don't receive.** He retains insights far better when he arrives at them himself. Being told the answer produces shallow understanding. Being asked the right question produces lasting understanding.
- **One thing at a time.** When multiple concepts are introduced together, he loses the thread. Keep sessions focused on a single problem.
- **Real examples over theory.** Performance metrics (fib(50) going from 60s → 0.037ms) made memoization click in a way that no explanation would have.
- **Honest feedback.** He doesn't need encouragement — he needs to know if he's right and why.

## What Motivates Him

- Understanding the *why*, not just the *how*
- Seeing real-world applicability (or correctly identifying when something isn't applicable)
- Mastering a pattern he can apply to new problems, not memorizing solutions
- Interview readiness — this is a concrete goal driving the curriculum

## Characteristic Behaviors

- Will catch his own bugs when asked the right question — often doesn't need to be told what's wrong
- Pushes back on impractical solutions (called out recursive string reversal as O(n²) and pointless)
- Asks clarifying questions when something doesn't make sense rather than proceeding blindly
- Prefers to write his own documentation before Claude creates the final version
- One problem per chat session — strict preference, self-imposed

## What to Watch

- Can conflate "it works" with "it's correct/optimal" — needs prompting to ask the next question
- Edge cases sometimes get skipped until pushed
- Complexity analysis needed explicit prompting early on — improving, but still probe for it
